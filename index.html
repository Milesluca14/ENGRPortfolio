About Me

Engineer & Builder

I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Connecticut, graduating in 2029, with a passion for transforming abstract concepts into tangible, high-performance systems. My approach combines rigorous analytical thinking with hands-on fabrication experience.

Whether designing aerodynamic components for our Formula SAE race car, machining precision parts on CNC equipment, or optimizing robotic mechanisms for competition, I thrive in environments where design meets manufacturing reality.

My technical foundation spans CAD modeling (SolidWorks, Fusion 360, AutoCAD), FEA simulation, manufacturing processes, and collaborative engineering workflows. Beyond technical skills, I've developed leadership capabilities through scouting (Eagle Scout candidate) and coordinating engineering team efforts.

I believe the best engineering happens at the intersection of theoretical knowledge and practical execution—where designs are validated through testing, failures become learning opportunities, and iteration drives excellence.
